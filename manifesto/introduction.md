# Mnemosyne Manifesto — Introduction

We thought the central challenge of artificial intelligence would be intelligence itself.

Could machines reason?  
Could they generate?  
Could they synthesize?  
Could they create?

Now, almost overnight, the world has discovered something extraordinary:

They can.

But while building multiple AI-native systems, a different problem began to emerge.

The systems could think.

But they could not reliably preserve why.

Why a decision had been made.  
Why an assumption changed.  
Why a constraint mattered.  
Why a prior lesson existed.  
Why an operational path had once been rejected.

The problem was no longer merely intelligence.

The problem was continuity.
